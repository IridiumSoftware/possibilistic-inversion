# Phase 5 triad brief — something feels off, want a witness pass

**Status:** real-data integration of `posdec` (possibilistic decomposition for
seismic tomography) on the Equinor Volve open dataset is at phase 5.
Phase 4 (1D Vp(z), single well) calibrated cleanly. Phase 5 (2D Vp(w,z),
joint two-well) did NOT, and the explanation I offered ("the prior
dominates where rays don't go") may be too neat. Want your read on
whether the methodology is genuinely working, genuinely failing, or
whether I'm comparing apples to oranges in the diagnostics themselves.

---

## Method recap (one paragraph)

`posdec` produces a *possibilistic* split of an ensemble of feasible models
into **forced-high / forced-low / forced-quiet / measure-dependent** per
cell — by comparing the per-cell anomaly interval [a_min, a_max] across the
ensemble to a deadband ±eps around zero. The ensemble is generated by
random-reference damped Gauss-Newton inversion: each member starts from a
smooth random Vp profile, the eikonal forward predicts travel times, the
GN step bisects lambda to land the data RMS near a noise target. Three
calibration tests: (a) **sonic-inside** = does the wireline log fall inside
the per-bin ensemble Vp interval, (b) **holdout-inside** = does an
80/20 held-out pick's observed time fall inside the per-pick ensemble
predicted-time interval, (c) **residual stats** vs the ensemble median.

## Phase 4 — 1D worked

15/9-F-15A walkaway VSP, 1215 picks, 1D Vp(z) on 70 m bins, 80-member
ensemble, eikonal forward.

  - sonic-inside (DT-EDIT): 36/45 (80.0%)
  - holdout-inside: 228/243 (93.8%)
  - holdout median residual: +58 ms
  - holdout ensemble interval width median: 200 ms
  - ensemble RMS median: 158 ms
  - decomposition: 22.2% forced-high + 37.8% forced-low + 40% measure-dep

## Phase 5 — 2D joint went off

Added 15/9-F-11 T2 walkaway VSP (816 picks, ~1000 m lateral from F-15A,
deep section 1741-4530 m). Joint 2031 picks, 2D Vp(w, z) on 40 m grid
(116 × 69 = 8004 cells), 12-member ensemble × 2 GN iter, same eikonal
forward.

  - sonic-inside at F-15A wellhead w-column: 8/79 (10.1%)
  - sonic-inside at F-11T2 wellhead w-column: 5/50 (10.0%)
  - joint holdout-inside: 94/413 (22.8%)
  - holdout median residual: +37 ms
  - holdout ensemble interval width median: **21 ms**
  - ensemble RMS median: 146 ms
  - decomposition: **79.2% forced-quiet**, 19.1% measure-dependent,
    1.7% forced-high+low combined

## The interpretation I offered

The 1D→2D expansion was ~180× model space (45 bins → 8004 cells) without
180× more data. The Tikhonov damping toward the smooth random reference
dominates cells with no ray illumination, so the ensemble interval per
cell collapses to ~prior-noise. The 79% forced-quiet zone IS the
prior-dominated zone; the 19% measure-dependent zone IS the
data-illuminated zone (where rays cross between the wells); the
calibration drop is the methodology *correctly* reporting that the
nominal coverage isn't earned at this prior strength. Same lesson as
Brian's ORSI Tier-1 sensitivity finding on the synthetic.

## What feels off

That narrative is too clean. Specific concerns I can't shake:

**1. Anomaly baseline is the ensemble mean's depth-trend.** With a
prior-dominated ensemble, the ensemble mean ≈ the prior trend, and
anomaly = member - trend ≈ 0 nearly everywhere. So "forced-quiet"
might be a **tautology** of construction, not a finding. If I changed
the baseline (e.g., to the wireline-sonic trend, or to a different
random draw), would the 79% number move?

**2. The per-well sonic validation samples a fixed w-column, but both
wells deviate ~1 km horizontally.** The sonic is logged along the bore,
so it samples Vp at the *bore's actual xy* at each depth, not at the
wellhead's xy. My validation compares ensemble(w=wellhead_w, z) to
sonic(MD=z) — these are at different lateral positions for the
deviated portion. How much of the 80% → 10% sonic-inside drop is
real methodology behavior vs. a mis-aligned comparison?

**3. Tight predicted-time interval (21 ms) with large residual (137 ms RMS).**
Phase 4 had wide interval (200 ms) and 93.8% holdout-inside. Phase 5
has narrow interval (21 ms) with 22.8% holdout-inside. Possible
readings:
  - (a) The ensemble agrees on the data-fitting answer (narrow
    interval) but the answer is wrong on held-out rays (large
    residual). This would be a *bias* problem from the
    parameterization — 2D Vp(w,z) isn't the right model for the real
    3D Earth.
  - (b) The ensemble is under-spreading because of prior dominance
    (the interpretation I offered) — but if that were the only
    explanation, the sonic at the well (where the data IS) should
    still cover sonic-truth, and it doesn't.
  - (c) Both, in different proportions.

**4. Did the data actually constrain the lateral structure?** With 32
unique-source grid cells over a 116-cell-wide model and ray-bundles
concentrated near each wellhead, the lateral aperture is small.
Maybe the inversion is effectively still 1D-per-w-column, with each
column being damped to the prior because cross-cell coupling is
weak.

## What I want from you

Independent reads on:

  - **Is the phase-5 calibration drop genuinely informative about the
    methodology**, or is it an artifact of one of the four concerns above?
  - **Which diagnostic do I actually trust** for the 2D case? The holdout
    inside-rate (22.8%) is calibration-shaped; the sonic-inside (10%) is
    a lateral-position-confounded measurement; the decomposition
    fractions (79% forced-quiet) reference a baseline that may be the
    ensemble's own prior.
  - **What's the smallest experiment that would tell us if it's "prior
    dominance" vs. "wrong parameterization"?** Looser prior (smaller
    envelope, weaker damping, longer correlation)? More GN iterations?
    Reference Vp(z) drawn from sonic statistics instead of generic trend?
    Use the wireline-sonic-fit as the anomaly baseline instead of the
    ensemble mean?
  - **Anything load-bearing I'm not seeing.**

I want to figure out whether phase 5 is recoverable with a different
parameterization / prior, or whether the negative-looking result is
the right report.
